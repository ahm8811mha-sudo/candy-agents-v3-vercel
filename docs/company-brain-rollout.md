# Company Brain rollout

## Phase A — foundation
- Knowledge graph and evidence-linked company memory.
- Decision recommendations with confidence, risks, and alternatives.
- Scenario simulation and digital-twin snapshots.
- Autonomous planning drafts that require activation.
- Skills registry with permissions and risk controls.

## Phase B — learning
- Outcome capture for decisions, projects, suppliers, customers, employees, and KPIs.
- Feature computation and model-quality tracking.
- Recommendation calibration from observed outcomes.
- Narrative BI that explains drivers and uncertainty.

## Phase C — controlled execution
- Bind approved plans to the durable workflow runtime.
- Require integration attempts, idempotency keys, receipts, reconciliation, and audit.
- Keep payments, government submissions, legal commitments, hiring/termination, and high-risk financial actions behind human approval.

## Phase D — commercial foundation
- Preserve tenant isolation in all intelligence tables and APIs.
- Add workspace-level capability policies and quotas.
- Introduce signed skill packages and review status before a marketplace launch.

The current installation remains a private single-owner workspace. Multi-company support is a structural capability, not an enabled onboarding surface.
